CHAPTER I

GENERAL PROVISIONS

Chapter I establishes the foundational framework of the Code of General Ordinances of Santa, Ilocos Sur. It defines the official title and scope of the Code, prescribes the governing rules for legal construction and interpretation, and provides technical definitions of terms commonly used throughout the municipal ordinance system.

Section 1A.01. Title

This Ordinance shall be known as the CODE OF GENERAL ORDINANCES OF SANTA, ILOCOS SUR.

This provision formally establishes the official designation of the municipal codified ordinance system adopted by the Municipality of Santa.

All chapters, articles and sections included in this Code collectively form part of the municipality’s local legislative framework and administrative governance system.

The title provision provides official legal identity to the codified ordinance system and serves as the primary reference for all municipal legislative enactments.

Section 1A.02. Scope

This Code covers all general and special ordinances enacted over the years by the Municipality of Santa, Ilocos Sur.

The scope provision clarifies that the Code is intended to consolidate, organize and harmonize municipal ordinances into a systematic and accessible legal framework.

Through codification, residents, businesses, public officials and stakeholders may efficiently locate, interpret and apply local laws, regulations and municipal procedures.

Official Codification

The Code of General Ordinances serves as the official codified compilation of municipal laws, regulations and legislative enactments of the Municipality of Santa.

Municipal Authority

The authority of the Code originates from the legislative powers granted to the Sangguniang Bayan under the Local Government Code and related national laws.

Public Accessibility

Codification improves accessibility and allows residents, businesses, officials and stakeholders to properly understand municipal laws and legal responsibilities.

Legal Note: Codification does not automatically create entirely new legislation. Instead, it consolidates and harmonizes previously enacted ordinances into a unified legal framework.

Section 1B.01. Words and Phrases

Words and phrases embodied in this Code but not specifically defined shall have the same meaning as found in legal dictionaries as well as in existing laws.

This provision ensures consistency in legal interpretation by requiring undefined terms to be understood according to accepted legal usage and jurisprudence.

Interpretation Rule: Undefined terms shall be interpreted according to recognized legal principles and statutory construction.

Section 1B.02. Construction of Codal Provisions

In construing the provisions of this Code, words and phrases shall be interpreted according to their common, technical or legal meaning.

  • General Rule — ordinary language usage shall generally apply.
  • Gender and Number — singular includes plural whenever applicable.
  • Computation of Time — the first day is excluded while the last day is included unless it falls on a holiday or Sunday.
  • References — references to chapters, articles and sections are deemed internal references unless otherwise specified.

Mandatory Terms

The term shall indicates mandatory compliance and legal obligation.

Permissive Terms

The term may indicates discretion or permissive authority.

Section 1C.01. Meaning of Technical Terms

The following terms shall be interpreted according to their technical and legal meaning as used throughout this Code.

Amusement

A pleasurable diversion, entertainment, recreation or form of enjoyment.

Amusement Places

Includes theaters, cinemas, concert halls, circuses and similar places intended for public entertainment.

Agricultural Product

Includes crops, vegetables, livestock, fishery products and related agricultural yields and by-products.

Business

A trade or commercial activity regularly engaged in as means of livelihood involving independent judgment.

Bars

Includes beer gardens and establishments where intoxicating liquors or malt beverages are sold for compensation.

Banks and Financial Institutions

Includes non-bank financial intermediaries, lending investors, finance companies, pawnshops and insurance companies.

Capital

The actual estate, money or property used by an individual or corporation in business.

Charges

Refers to pecuniary liabilities such as rents or fees imposed upon persons or property.

Corporation

Includes partnerships, associations and juridical entities organized under law.

Fee

A charge fixed by law or ordinance for the regulation or inspection of business activity.

Franchise

A right or privilege conferred upon persons or corporations under conditions imposed by government.

Gross Sales or Receipts

The total amount of money or equivalent representing contract price, compensation or services received.

Levy

An imposition or collection of an assessment, tax, tribute or fine.

License or Permit

A right or permission granted by competent authority to engage in business or occupation.

Local Government

Includes provinces, cities, municipalities and barangays.

Manufacturer

Any person engaged in processing or altering raw materials into finished products for sale or distribution.

Occupation

One’s regular business, trade, profession or employment.

Operator

The owner, manager or person responsible for operating a business establishment.

Profession

A calling requiring government board or bar examinations such as law or engineering.

Revenue

Taxes, fees and charges collected for public purposes by the municipality.

Tax

An enforced monetary contribution levied by law to support governmental needs.

Vessel

Every type of boat, craft or artificial contrivance used for transportation on water.

Wharfage

A fee assessed against the cargo of a vessel engaged in trade.

Wholesale

A sale where commodities are bought for resale rather than direct end user consumption.
