ARTICLE G

Special Sessions

Special sessions may be called by the Municipal Mayor or by a majority of the members of the Sangguniang Bayan whenever public interest, municipal necessity, or urgent legislative action requires immediate deliberation.

Written notice of special sessions stating the date, time, place, and subject matter to be considered shall be served upon all members of the Sangguniang Bayan.

Only matters specified in the notice of special session may be considered unless all members present unanimously agree otherwise.

ARTICLE H

Order of Business

The Order of Business during sessions shall follow the parliamentary sequence approved by the Sangguniang Bayan to ensure orderly legislative proceedings.

The minutes of the previous session shall be read, corrected when necessary, and approved before proceeding to unfinished business.

Matters pending before the Sangguniang Bayan shall be included in the Calendar of Business in accordance with legislative priority and parliamentary procedure.

ARTICLE I

Committees

Standing Committees shall be organized by the Sangguniang Bayan for the efficient study, review, and recommendation of legislative measures affecting municipal governance.

Proposed ordinances, resolutions, and legislative matters may be referred to the appropriate committee for study, recommendation, and report.

Committees shall submit written reports and recommendations to the Sangguniang Bayan after conducting necessary hearings, investigations, and consultations.

ARTICLE J

Rules of Debate

No member shall speak or address the body unless recognized by the Presiding Officer.

Members shall observe courtesy, parliamentary discipline, and mutual respect during all legislative debates and deliberative proceedings.

Debate may be limited by motion duly approved by the Sangguniang Bayan whenever necessary to facilitate orderly proceedings.

ARTICLE K

Voting Procedures

Unless otherwise provided by law, a majority vote of the members present constituting a quorum shall be necessary for the approval of ordinances, resolutions, and legislative actions.

Roll-call voting may be conducted whenever required by law, parliamentary rules, or upon motion approved by the Sangguniang Bayan.

Members having direct pecuniary interest in matters under consideration shall abstain from voting in accordance with law and ethical standards.

ARTICLE L

Ordinances and Resolutions

Proposed ordinances shall undergo prescribed readings, committee review, deliberation, and approval before enactment by the Sangguniang Bayan.

Resolutions expressing the sense, policy, or administrative position of the Sangguniang Bayan shall be approved in accordance with parliamentary procedure.

ARTICLE M

Discipline and Decorum

Members of the Sangguniang Bayan shall conduct themselves in a manner consistent with ethical standards, parliamentary discipline, and the dignity of public office.

The Sangguniang Bayan may impose disciplinary measures upon members for disorderly conduct, unethical behavior, or violation of parliamentary procedures in accordance with law.

The Presiding Officer shall preserve order and decorum during sessions and may take necessary action to maintain orderly proceedings.

ARTICLE N

Public Participation

Public hearings may be conducted whenever required by law or whenever the Sangguniang Bayan determines that public consultation is necessary for proposed legislative measures.

Citizens shall be afforded reasonable opportunity to express views, recommendations, and concerns on matters affecting municipal governance and public welfare.

Legislative proceedings of the Sangguniang Bayan shall remain open to the public except when executive sessions are authorized by law or parliamentary rules.

ARTICLE O

Amendments and Revisions

Amendments to these Internal Rules of Procedure may be proposed by any member of the Sangguniang Bayan and approved in accordance with parliamentary procedure.

Revisions to these rules may be undertaken whenever necessary to improve legislative efficiency, administrative continuity, and parliamentary operations.

ARTICLE P

Applicability Clause

The provisions of the Local Government Code, related national laws, administrative issuances, and parliamentary authorities shall suppletorily apply whenever not inconsistent with these Internal Rules of Procedure.

EFFECTIVITY

Final Effectivity

These Internal Rules of Procedure shall take effect immediately upon approval and adoption by the Sangguniang Bayan of Santa, Ilocos Sur.
