Article A — The Legislative Body

The Sangguniang Bayan serves as the legislative body of the municipality and exercises local legislative authority in accordance with the Local Government Code and municipal ordinances.

The local legislative power shall be exercised by the Sangguniang Bayan of this municipality.

The Sangguniang Bayan shall be composed of the Municipal Vice-Mayor as Presiding Officer, the regular Sangguniang Bayan Members, the President of the Liga ng mga Barangay, and the President of the Pambayang Pederasyon ng mga Sangguniang Kabataan.

The term of office of the Members of the Sangguniang Bayan of Santa, Ilocos Sur shall be three (3) years commencing on such date as may be provided by law.

All members of the Sangguniang Bayan of Santa, Ilocos Sur shall, upon assumption to office, take an oath of office or affirmation in the prescribed form before a person authorized to administer oaths. Copies of the oath or affirmation shall be filed and preserved in the Office of the Municipal Mayor and the Office of the Sangguniang Bayan.

Article B — Presiding Officer

The Presiding Officer leads legislative proceedings, maintains parliamentary order, and supervises compliance with the internal rules of procedure.

The Municipal Vice-Mayor shall serve as the Presiding Officer of the Sangguniang Bayan.

  • Preside over sessions of the Sangguniang Bayan.
  • Preserve order and decorum during sessions.
  • Decide questions of order subject to appeal.
  • Sign ordinances, resolutions, writs, warrants, and subpoenas.
  • Exercise powers and functions provided by law.
  • Assist in expediting legislative business.
  • Vote only to break a tie.
  • Enforce compliance with internal rules and procedures.

Article C — Temporary Presiding Officer

A Temporary Presiding Officer may exercise legislative authority whenever the regular Presiding Officer is absent during sessions.

A Temporary Presiding Officer shall be elected from among the members of the Sangguniang Bayan whenever the regular Presiding Officer is absent during sessions.

Members present constituting a quorum shall elect from among themselves a Temporary Presiding Officer during the absence of the regular Presiding Officer.

The Temporary Presiding Officer shall certify enacted ordinances and adopted resolutions within ten (10) days after passage.

While presiding, the Temporary Presiding Officer shall have the authority to break a tie vote.

The Temporary Presiding Officer may temporarily relinquish the chair in order to participate in deliberations as a regular member.

Article D — The Sangguniang Bayan

This article governs the duties, responsibilities, discipline, voting obligations, and ethical standards of members of the Sangguniang Bayan.

Members of the Sangguniang Bayan shall assume office in the manner provided by law and shall represent the interests of the entire municipality. Members shall participate in legislative duties, attend sessions, vote on matters unless disqualified by direct pecuniary interest, and sign enacted ordinances and adopted resolutions.

Legislative Accountability

Members may be disciplined for disorderly conduct, unjustified absences, or violations of legislative rules and ethics.

Article E — The Secretary

The Secretary maintains legislative records, ordinances, minutes, archives, and official documentation of the Sangguniang Bayan.

The Sangguniang Secretary shall be appointed by the Vice-Mayor in accordance with Civil Service laws, rules, and regulations.

  • Attend sessions and keep official minutes.
  • Record ordinances and resolutions.
  • Forward legislative measures for review and approval.
  • Maintain the official seal and legislative archives.
  • Translate ordinances into the local dialect.
  • Provide certified copies of records upon request.
  • Assist members in drafting legislative measures.

Article F — Sessions

This article governs inaugural sessions, regular sessions, special sessions, quorum procedures, attendance, public participation, and adjournment protocols.

The Presiding Officer shall call for an inaugural session within five (5) working days upon assumption to office of the members.

Regular sessions shall be held every Monday at such time and place as formally designated by the Sangguniang Bayan.

Special sessions may be called when public interest so demands by the Municipal Mayor or by a majority of the members of the Sangguniang Bayan.

The Vice-Mayor may invite public officials to assist legislative deliberations in aid of legislation.

The Presiding Officer shall formally open sessions through a call to order followed by silent prayer, meditation, or invocation.

Members are required to attend all sessions unless prevented by valid circumstances duly reported to the Sangguniang Bayan.

Sessions shall remain open to the public unless a closed session is approved for reasons involving public interest, security, confidentiality, morality, or legislative dignity.

The exact opening and adjournment times of sessions shall be recorded in the official minutes.

Article G — Roll Call and Quorum

This article governs quorum requirements, attendance procedures, adjournment rules, legislative communication standards, and official session schedules of the Sangguniang Bayan.

A majority of all elected and qualified members of the Sangguniang Bayan shall constitute a quorum for the transaction of official business. The Municipal Vice-Mayor shall be considered a regular member for purposes of determining quorum.

Legislative Quorum Enforcement

If quorum is questioned during a session, the Presiding Officer shall immediately conduct a roll call and announce the results. Where there is no quorum, the Presiding Officer may declare a recess or adjournment until quorum is constituted.

After completion of roll call, a motion to adjourn may be entertained and approved by majority vote of members present.

No two (2) regular or special sessions shall be conducted within a single day.

Article H — Committees

Standing Committees and Special Committees assist the Sangguniang Bayan in reviewing legislative measures, conducting hearings, and supervising specialized areas of municipal governance.

Committee on Appropriations

Handles budgets, appropriations, compensation systems, and allocation of municipal funds.

Committee on Ways and Means

Handles taxes, fees, revenue measures, and revenue ordinance codification.

Committee on Rules and Privileges

Handles legislative rules, parliamentary procedures, and member conduct.

Committee on Public Order and Safety

Handles peace and order, police matters, traffic regulations, and human rights concerns.

Committee on Environment and Natural Resources

Handles environmental protection, conservation, beautification, and ecological management.

Committee on Education, Culture and Tourism

Handles educational development, cultural affairs, and tourism promotion.

Committee on Women, Children and Family

Handles welfare, rights, and privileges of women, children, and families.

Committee on Agriculture and Livelihood

Handles agriculture, fisheries, cooperatives, accreditation, and livelihood systems.

Committee on Health and Sanitation

Handles public health, sanitation, and social welfare matters.

Committee on Public Works and Infrastructure

Handles roads, buildings, drainage, zoning, settlement planning, and infrastructure systems.

Committee on Youth and Sports Development

Handles youth welfare, sports development, and recreational programs.

Committee on Barangay Affairs

Handles barangay creation, barangay boundary issues, and barangay governance matters.

Committee on Trade and Industry

Handles trade, public market operations, slaughterhouse management, and local enterprises.

Committee on Labor and Employment

Handles labor relations and employment matters.

Committee on Elderly and Religious Affairs

Handles concerns involving senior citizens and religious affairs.

Committee on Legal Matters and Good Government

Handles legal concerns, ethics, accountability, and governance standards.

Each Standing Committee shall have a Chairman and at least two (2) members elected by majority vote of the Sangguniang Bayan.

No Sangguniang Bayan Member shall chair more than two (2) Standing Committees. Members with direct and substantial interests shall not participate in committee actions involving such interests.

The Sangguniang Bayan may organize Special Committees whenever necessary through majority vote.

Vacancies within committees shall be filled through election by the Sangguniang Bayan.

Committees shall meet at least once every month and may conduct hearings in public buildings.

Committee reports shall contain findings, conclusions, recommendations, signatures of concurring members, and minutes of committee hearings.

This article governs the official order of business, privilege hour, question hour, legislative calendars, and deliberation sequencing during sessions.

Minutes of each session shall be submitted for approval and signed by members, the Presiding Officer, and the Secretary.

Unfinished Business

Business pending upon adjournment of a previous session.

Business for the Day

Measures scheduled for consideration during the session.

Unassigned Business

Measures not yet referred or calendared for committee action.

The calendar of business shall be distributed during every session unless no additions have been made to previously distributed business calendars.

Article J — Ordinances and Resolutions

This article governs ordinance enactment, resolutions, legislative readings, committee referrals, legislative voting, authorship, and procedural enactment requirements.

Legislative Character

Permanent legislative actions shall be enacted through ordinances while temporary or proprietary matters shall be acted upon through resolutions.

Legislative Form Requirements

All proposed ordinances and resolutions shall be in writing with assigned numbers, titles, clauses, and effectivity provisions.

Committee Review

Measures shall generally undergo committee review prior to second reading unless certified urgent.

Voting Requirement

Measures shall require majority approval where quorum exists.

All ordinances and resolutions enacted by the Sangguniang Bayan shall be forwarded to the Mayor for approval or veto within the period prescribed by law.

During first reading, only the title of the measure shall be read before referral to the appropriate committee.

Second reading shall involve sponsorship, full reading, debate, amendments, and parliamentary motions.

During third reading, no amendments or debates shall be entertained and final voting shall immediately proceed.

Committees shall submit reports within thirty (30) days after referral of measures.

Erroneous committee referrals may be corrected through majority vote or committee motion.

Authors of consolidated or substituted measures shall be recognized according to legislative filing and authorship records.

Article K — Veto Power

This article governs the veto authority of the Municipal Mayor, veto override procedures, urgent legislative measures, and effectivity of vetoed ordinances and resolutions.

The Municipal Mayor may veto ordinances or resolutions by returning the measure with written objections within ten (10) days from receipt. Failure to return the measure within the prescribed period shall result in automatic approval.

Partial Veto Authority

The Mayor may veto particular items involving appropriations or financial liabilities without affecting the remaining valid provisions.

The Sangguniang Bayan may override the Mayor’s veto through a two-thirds (2/3) vote of all members. Once overridden, the ordinance or resolution shall become valid and effective even without the Mayor’s approval.

Legislative matters certified urgent by the Mayor may immediately be presented and considered by the body without the need for suspension of procedural rules.

Urgent Matters

Urgent matters are those involving substantial public interest where delay may prejudice essential governmental operations or services.

Article L — Effectivity and Posting

This article governs ordinance effectivity, posting requirements, publication procedures, translations, and enforcement limitations for invalidated measures.

Unless otherwise stated, ordinances and resolutions shall take effect after ten (10) days from posting.

Copies of enacted ordinances and adopted resolutions shall be posted at the Municipal Hall and at least two (2) conspicuous public places within the municipality.

Penal Ordinances

Ordinances containing penal sanctions shall additionally be published in a newspaper of general circulation where available.

Translation Requirement

The Secretary shall translate ordinances into the dialect commonly used by the majority of the municipality’s residents.

Attempting to enforce ordinances, resolutions, or executive orders already disapproved or invalidated may constitute grounds for suspension or dismissal of responsible officials or employees.

Article M — Suspension of Rules

This article governs the suspension of internal procedural rules during legislative sessions.

Any portion of the Internal Rules of Procedure, except provisions prescribed by law, may be suspended during a session through general consent or by two-thirds (2/3) vote of members constituting a quorum.

Article N — Amendments

This article governs amendments to the Internal Rules of Procedure of the Sangguniang Bayan.

The Internal Rules of Procedure may be amended during any regular session through a two-thirds (2/3) vote of all members, provided prior notice has been given.

Limitation on Amendments

No provision based upon existing laws may be amended through internal procedural rules.

Article O — Supplementary Rules

This article establishes supplementary parliamentary authorities and legislative references applicable to the Sangguniang Bayan.

The parliamentary rules, procedures, and legislative practices of Philippine legislative bodies shall serve as supplementary authorities whenever not inconsistent with these Internal Rules of Procedure.

Supplementary Authorities

References to parliamentary writings and legislative procedural authorities may be used to supplement unresolved procedural matters.

Article P — Applicability Clause

This article incorporates national issuances and Local Government Code regulations into the legislative framework of the municipality.

Rules and regulations implementing the Local Government Code of 1991 and other national issuances pertaining to local legislation are hereby incorporated as integral parts of this Chapter.

Article Q — Effectivity

This article governs the final effectivity clause of the Internal Rules of Procedure.

These Internal Rules of Procedure shall take effect upon adoption by the Sangguniang Bayan.
