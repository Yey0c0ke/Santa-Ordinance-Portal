ARTICLE A

The Legislative Body

The local legislative power shall be exercised by the Sangguniang Bayan of this municipality.

The Sangguniang Bayan shall be composed of:

Municipal Vice-Mayor

Serves as the Presiding Officer of the Sangguniang Bayan.

Regular Members

Duly elected Sangguniang Bayan Members exercising legislative authority.

Liga President

President of the Liga ng mga Barangay serving as member of the body.

SK Federation

President of the Pambayang Pederasyon ng Sangguniang Kabataan.

The term of office of the Members of the Sangguniang Bayan of Santa, Ilocos Sur shall be three (3) years which shall commence on such date as may be provided by law.

All members of the Sangguniang Bayan of Santa, Ilocos Sur shall, upon assumption to office, take an oath of office or affirmation in the prescribed form, duly subscribed before a person authorized to administer oath.

Copies of the oath of affirmation shall be filed and preserved in the Office of the Municipal Mayor and the Office of the Sangguniang Bayan of Santa, Ilocos Sur.

ARTICLE B

Presiding Officer

The Municipal Vice-Mayor shall be the Presiding Officer of the Sangguniang Bayan.

Session Leadership

Preside over the sessions of the Sangguniang Bayan.

Order and Decorum

Preserve order and decorum during sessions and take necessary measures in cases of disturbance.

Parliamentary Authority

Decide questions of order subject to appeal by any Sangguniang Bayan Member.

Legislative Signing

Sign all acts, ordinances, resolutions, memorials, writs, warrants, and subpoenas.

Administrative Authority

Exercise such other powers and perform functions as may be provided by law, ordinance, or regulation.

Legislative Facilitation

Assist in expediting the business of the Sangguniang Bayan through procedural guidance.

Tie-Breaking Vote

Vote only to break a tie and cannot vote to create a tie.

Internal Rules Enforcement

Enforce compliance with the Internal Rules of Procedures of the Sangguniang Bayan.

ARTICLE C

Temporary Presiding Officer

There shall be a Temporary Presiding Officer to be elected from among the members of the Sangguniang Bayan.

The Temporary Presiding Officer, without losing his status as a regular member including his rights and prerogatives, shall temporarily exercise all the powers, duties and functions of the Presiding Officer whenever the latter is absent during sessions.

In the event of the inability of the regular Presiding Officer to preside over a session, the members present constituting a quorum shall elect from among themselves a Temporary Presiding Officer.

The Temporary Presiding Officer, as the case may be, shall certify within ten (10) days from the passage of ordinances enacted or resolutions adopted by the Sangguniang Bayan in session.

While presiding, the Temporary Presiding Officer shall have the right to break a tie.

The Temporary Presiding Officer may, on his own discretion, relinquish the chair temporarily to any member of the Sangguniang Bayan if he desires to participate in deliberations in his capacity as a regular member.

ARTICLE D

The Sangguniang Bayan

Members of the Sangguniang Bayan shall assume office on the day and in the manner provided by law, and shall hold office until their successors shall have been duly elected, chosen, and qualified.

Members shall meet at the designated place for the holding of sessions and proceed to legislative business in accordance with law and parliamentary rules.

Financial Disclosure

Every member shall make full disclosure of financial and business interests as required under the Rules and Regulations Implementing the Local Government Code of 1991.

Legislative Conduct

Members may be disciplined for disorderly conduct, disruptive behavior, or repeated unjustified absences during sessions.

Public Representation

Members shall represent the interests of the people of the entire municipality in all legislative matters.

Voting Responsibility

Every member present shall vote on every question unless disqualified by direct personal or pecuniary interest.

Every member shall sign every page of enacted ordinances and adopted resolutions respectively.

Absences for four (4) consecutive sessions without justifiable cause may subject a member to:

  • Censure
  • Reprimand
  • Exclusion from sessions
  • Suspension for not more than sixty (60) days
  • Expulsion from the Sangguniang Bayan

Suspension or expulsion shall require concurrence of at least two-thirds (2/3) vote of all Sangguniang Bayan Members.

A member convicted by final judgment to imprisonment of at least one (1) year for any crime involving moral turpitude shall be expelled from the Sangguniang Bayan.

ARTICLE E

The Secretary

The Sangguniang Secretary shall be appointed by the Vice-Mayor.

Compensation, allowances, and other emoluments shall be determined by law, ordinance, or regulation.

Appointment of the Sangguniang Secretary shall be in accordance with Civil Service law, rules, and regulations.

Session Records

Attend sessions and keep the minutes of all proceedings of the Sangguniang Bayan.

Ordinance Registry

Record all ordinances and resolutions enacted or adopted by the Sangguniang Bayan.

Transmission of Measures

Forward ordinances and resolutions to the Municipal Mayor for appropriate action.

Official Seal

Keep and affix the official Sangguniang seal on approved legislative measures.

Provincial Review

Forward approved ordinances and resolutions to the Sangguniang Panlalawigan within the prescribed period.

Certification

Attest to all ordinances and resolutions approved by the Sangguniang Bayan and signed by the Municipal Mayor.

Translation Duties

Translate ordinances and resolutions into the dialect used by the majority of the people of the municipality.

Public Access

Keep records open for public inspection during regular business hours except those classified as confidential.

Certified Copies

Furnish certified copies of records and documents upon request and payment of prescribed fees.

Archives Custodian

Act as custodian of the public library and archives, if any, and account for properties therein.

Legislative Support

Assist Sangguniang Bayan Members in preparation of draft ordinances, resolutions, and official documents.

Other Functions

Exercise such other duties and functions as may be prescribed by law or ordinance.

ARTICLE F

Sessions

The Presiding Officer shall call for an inaugural session of the Sangguniang Bayan within five (5) working days upon assumption to office of its members.

Regular sessions of the Sangguniang Bayan shall be conducted every Monday at such time and place as stipulated in the formal notice thereof or as may be decided upon in the immediately preceding session.

When public interest so demands, the Municipal Mayor or a majority of the members of the Sangguniang Bayan may call special sessions.

Written notice stating the date, time, and purpose of the session shall be served personally or left at the usual place of residence of the member at least twenty-four (24) hours before the special session is held.

Unless otherwise agreed upon by two-thirds (2/3) vote of the members present, no other matter may be considered except those stated in the notice.

In order to expedite the enactment of resolutions and ordinances, the Vice-Mayor may invite public officials to assist the Sangguniang Bayan for legislative purposes.

The Presiding Officer shall open sessions by calling the Sangguniang Bayan to order.

A one-minute silent prayer, meditation, or invocation by a designated member may precede official proceedings.

Every member of the Sangguniang Bayan shall attend all sessions unless expressly excluded or prevented by sickness or other unavoidable circumstances duly reported through the Secretary.

Sessions or committee meetings shall be open to the public unless a closed session is ordered by majority vote in the public interest or for reasons involving security, decency, morality, confidentiality, or the dignity of the Sangguniang Bayan.

Upon approval of a motion to hold a closed session, the Presiding Officer shall direct the clearing of the session hall and closing of doors.

The exact hour of opening and adjournment of sessions shall be entered into the official minutes.

ARTICLE G

Roll Call and Quorum

A majority of all members of the Sangguniang Bayan who have been elected and qualified shall constitute a quorum to transact official business.

The Municipal Vice-Mayor shall be included in the determination of a quorum as a regular member of the Sangguniang Bayan.

Should a question of quorum be raised during a session, the Presiding Officer shall immediately call the roll of members and announce the results.

In the absence of a quorum, the Presiding Officer may declare a recess until a quorum is constituted.

A majority of members present may adjourn from hour to hour and compel the attendance of absent members without justifiable cause.

If no quorum is still constituted despite remedial actions, no business shall be transacted and the session shall be adjourned for lack of quorum.

The official medium of communication before the Sangguniang Bayan shall be:

  • Filipino
  • English
  • Ilokano

Regular sessions shall be held every Monday at 9:00 o'clock in the morning.

If the regular session falls on a holiday, the same shall be held on the following day.

Sessions shall be held at the Sangguniang Bayan Hall of Santa, Ilocos Sur or in any public place agreed upon by the body.

At any time after the roll call has been completed, a motion to adjourn shall be in order.

If approved by a majority of those present, all proceedings shall be vacated.

No two (2) sessions, whether regular or special, may be held within a single day.

ARTICLE H

Committees

The Sangguniang Bayan shall elect Chairpersons and Members of standing committees with specific legislative jurisdictions.

Appropriations

Budget approvals, appropriations, personnel compensation, reorganization, projects, and services.

Ways and Means

Taxes, fees, charges, loans, and revenue generation systems.

Rules and Privileges

Internal procedures, parliamentary order, and member privileges.

Public Order and Safety

Peace and order, traffic, fire prevention, jail management, and human rights.

Additional standing committees shall include Environment, Education, Women and Family, Agriculture, Health, Public Works, Youth and Sports, Barangay Affairs, Trade and Industry, Labor, Elderly Affairs, and Legal Matters.

Each standing committee shall have a Chairperson and at least two (2) members elected by majority vote of the Sangguniang Bayan.

No Sangguniang Bayan Member shall chair more than two (2) standing committees.

Committees shall meet at least once a month, and a majority of all committee members shall constitute a quorum.

Committee reports shall contain findings, recommendations, committee actions, and minutes of hearings or meetings.

ARTICLE I

Order and Calendar of Business

The Order of Business for every session of the Sangguniang Bayan shall be:

  • Call to Order
  • Invocation
  • Roll Call
  • Reading and Approval of Minutes
  • Privilege Hour
  • Question Hour
  • First Reading and Referral of Measures
  • Committee Reports
  • Calendar of Business
  • Announcements
  • Adjournment

The Privilege Hour shall be the allotted period for members of the Sangguniang Bayan to express sentiments, positions, constructive criticisms, and observations on matters of public interest.

Members delivering privilege speeches shall furnish copies thereof to the Presiding Officer, fellow members, and the Secretary, unless delivered extemporaneously.

Minutes of each session shall be submitted to the Sangguniang Bayan for approval.

The original approved copy shall be signed by the members, Presiding Officer, and the Secretary.

Unfinished Business

Matters under consideration at the time of adjournment whose deliberation shall resume until disposition.

Business for the Day

Ordinances, resolutions, and legislative measures calendared for consideration during the session.

Unassigned Business

Measures reported out by committees but not yet calendared or referred for final action.

ARTICLE J

Ordinances and Resolutions

Legislative actions of a general and permanent character shall be enacted in the form of ordinances, while temporary matters shall be passed in the form of resolutions.

Proposed ordinances and resolutions shall be in writing and shall contain an assigned number, title, enacting clause, and date of effectivity.

Every proposed ordinance shall be accompanied by a brief explanatory note containing justification for approval.

First Reading

Proposed ordinances and resolutions shall be reported by the Secretary and referred to the appropriate committee after reading of title only.

Second Reading

Measures shall be sponsored, debated, amended, and subjected to parliamentary motions during second reading.

Third Reading

No debate or amendment shall be allowed during third reading and the question upon passage shall immediately be taken.

Committee Referral

No ordinance or resolution shall proceed to second reading unless reported out by the proper committee or certified urgent.

No ordinance or resolution shall be valid unless approved by a majority of members present, there being a quorum.

Ordinances or resolutions authorizing payment of money or creating liability shall require affirmative vote of a majority of all Sangguniang Bayan Members.

Upon request of any member, the "ayes" and "nays" shall be recorded in the official minutes.

Every ordinance or resolution enacted or adopted by the Sangguniang Bayan shall be forwarded to the Municipal Mayor for approval.

If approved, the Mayor shall affix his signature on every page thereof and below the word "Approved" on the final page.

If the Mayor neither approves nor vetoes the ordinance or resolution within ten (10) days after receipt, the measure shall be deemed approved.

Committees shall submit reports on proposed ordinances or resolutions within thirty (30) days from referral.

Favorable committee reports shall be included in the Calendar of Business for legislative consideration.

Erroneous committee referrals may be corrected through proper motion or upon action of the Presiding Officer.

ARTICLE K

Veto Power

The Mayor may veto any ordinance or resolution presented to him by signifying his disapproval in writing and returning the same to the Sangguniang Bayan within ten (10) days from receipt thereof.

Failure to return the ordinance or resolution within the prescribed period shall result in the measure being deemed approved.

The Mayor may veto particular items of appropriation ordinances or measures directing payment of money or creating liability.

Upon receipt of the Mayor's veto, the Sangguniang Bayan shall record the objections in the official minutes and may reconsider the ordinance, resolution, or vetoed items.

The veto may be overridden by a two-thirds (2/3) vote of all members of the Sangguniang Bayan.

Upon successful override, the ordinance or resolution shall become valid and effective even without the Mayor's approval or signature.

Any legislative matter duly certified by the Mayor as urgent may, without need of suspending the rules, be presented and considered by the body during the same meeting.

An urgent matter is one involving great public interest, the delay of which would prejudice essential government activities.

ARTICLE L

Effectivity and Posting

Unless otherwise stated, ordinances or resolutions shall take effect after the lapse of ten (10) days from the date of posting.

Copies of ordinances and resolutions shall be posted on the municipal bulletin board and in at least two (2) conspicuous public places.

Ordinances with penal sanctions shall likewise be published in a newspaper of general circulation whenever available.

The Secretary shall certify the fact of posting and shall cause translation of ordinances into the dialect used by the majority of the people of the municipality.

Any attempt to enforce, promulgate, or execute any ordinance, resolution, or executive order after disapproval by the Mayor or invalidation by the Sangguniang Panlalawigan shall constitute sufficient ground for suspension or dismissal of the responsible official or employee.

ARTICLE M

Suspension of Rules

Any part of the Internal Rules of Procedures, except those prescribed by existing laws, may be suspended at any particular session by general consent or by two-thirds (2/3) vote of the members present constituting a quorum.

ARTICLE N

Amendments

The Internal Rules of Procedures may be amended at any regular session by a two-thirds (2/3) vote of all members of the Sangguniang Bayan.

Prior notice of proposed amendments shall be given to all members of the Sangguniang Bayan.

No provision based on or prescribed by existing laws shall be amended.

ARTICLE O

Supplementary Rules

The rules, procedures, and parliamentary practices of legislative bodies in the Philippines, including recognized parliamentary authorities, shall serve as supplementary authorities of the Sangguniang Bayan insofar as they are not incompatible with the adopted Internal Rules of Procedures.

ARTICLE P

Applicability Clause

The Rules and Regulations implementing the Local Government Code of 1991 and other issuances of national government agencies pertaining to local legislation are hereby made integral parts of this chapter.

FINAL CLAUSE

Effectivity

These Internal Rules of Procedures shall take effect on the date of their adoption.
