Article A — Declaration of Policy

This Article establishes the municipal government’s commitment to ethical public service, accountability, integrity, professionalism, and public-interest-centered governance.

It is the policy of this municipal government to promote a high standard of ethics in public service. All local officials and employees shall at all times be accountable to the people and shall discharge their duties with utmost responsibility, integrity, competence and loyalty; act with patriotism and justice; lead modest lives and uphold public interest over personal interest.

Public Officials

Include elective and appointive officials and employees, permanent or temporary, whether in the career or non-career service, including military and police personnel, whether or not they receive compensation.

Gift

Refers to a thing or right disposed of gratuitously and includes simulated sales or liberality, excluding unsolicited gifts of nominal value not connected with favors.

Receiving Any Gift

Includes accepting directly or indirectly any gift beyond nominal value in anticipation of or in exchange for a favor.

Loan

Covers simple loans, commodatum, guarantees, financing arrangements, or accommodations intended to ensure approval.

Substantial Stockholder

Means a person who directly or indirectly owns sufficient shares to elect a director of a corporation.

Family of Local Officials or Employees

Refers to spouses and unmarried children below eighteen (18) years of age.

Conflict of Interest

Arises when private business interests conflict with the faithful performance of official duties.

Divestment

Means the transfer or disposal of interest in property by completely depriving oneself of title or ownership.

Relatives

Include persons related within the fourth civil degree of consanguinity or affinity including bilas, pakaisa, and balae.

Article B — Norms of Conduct of Local Officials and Employees

This Article establishes the standards of personal conduct, professionalism, neutrality, accountability, patriotism, and ethical public service expected from all local officials and employees.

Commitment to Public Interest

Government resources and powers shall be used efficiently, honestly, effectively, and economically in service of the public.

Professionalism

Officials and employees shall discharge duties with excellence, intelligence, competence, and skill.

Fairness and Sincerity

Officials shall act with justness and sincerity, avoid discrimination, and refrain from unlawful or immoral acts.

Political Neutrality

Public service shall be rendered without unfair discrimination regardless of political affiliation.

Responsiveness to the Public

Officials shall provide prompt, courteous, transparent, and efficient public service while avoiding red tape.

Nationalism and Patriotism

Officials shall promote Filipino interests, sovereignty, and locally produced resources and technologies.

Commitment to Democracy

Officials shall uphold constitutional principles, public accountability, and civilian supremacy.

Simple Living

Officials and employees shall lead modest lives appropriate to their positions and lawful income.

Article C — Duties and Responsibilities of Local Officials and Employees

This Article establishes the operational obligations, administrative responsibilities, responsiveness standards, and document-processing duties of local officials and employees.
  • Act promptly on letters, requests, and official communications within fifteen (15) working days.
  • Submit annual performance reports within forty-five (45) working days after the end of the year.
  • Process official papers and documents expeditiously and efficiently.
  • Attend promptly to public transactions and requests for government services.
  • Ensure accessibility of public documents for inspection during reasonable working hours.

Administrative Accountability

The Article institutionalizes responsiveness, transparency, efficiency, and procedural accountability within municipal governance operations.

Article D — Ethical Standards

This Article governs prohibited acts, conflict-related transactions, misuse of confidential information, solicitation of gifts, and ethical restrictions imposed upon local officials and employees.

Financial and Material Interest

Officials and employees shall not possess financial or material interests in transactions requiring approval of their office.

Outside Employment

Officials shall not manage or accept employment in private enterprises regulated or supervised by their office unless authorized by law.

Practice of Profession

Practice of profession is prohibited where conflicts with official duties may arise, subject to existing laws.

Recommendation to Private Enterprises

Officials shall not recommend persons to private entities with pending or regular transactions with their office.

Disclosure of Confidential Information

Confidential or classified information shall not be used for private gain or to prejudice public interest.

Solicitation or Acceptance of Gifts

Officials and employees shall not solicit or accept gifts, favors, loans, gratuities, or entertainment connected with official duties.

Foreign Government Gifts

Acceptance of nominal souvenirs, scholarships, medical treatment, and approved travel grants from foreign governments may be allowed under lawful conditions.

Article E — Transparency in Public Service

This Article establishes transparency mechanisms, SALN requirements, disclosure obligations, accessibility of public documents, and safeguards against misuse of government records.

All local officials and employees, except those serving in honorary capacity, laborers, casuals, or temporary employees, shall file under oath their Statement of Assets, Liabilities and Net Worth (SALN), including disclosure of business interests and financial connections.

Real Property

Includes improvements, acquisition costs, assessed value, and fair market value.

Personal Property

Includes movable assets and acquisition costs.

Other Assets

Includes investments, bank deposits, stocks, bonds, and similar financial interests.

Liabilities

All financial obligations and indebtedness must be declared.

Business Interests and Financial Connections

All business interests and financial relationships of officials and qualified family members must be disclosed.

Mandatory Filing Periods

SALN and disclosure documents shall be filed within thirty (30) days after assumption of office, on or before April 30 annually, and within thirty (30) days after separation from service.

Every local official or employee shall identify and disclose relatives in government in the form and manner prescribed by the Civil Service Commission.

  • Statements filed under this Chapter shall be available for inspection during reasonable hours.
  • Statements may be reproduced after ten (10) working days from filing.
  • Reasonable reproduction and certification fees may be charged.
  • Statements shall remain publicly accessible for ten (10) years.

It shall be unlawful to obtain or use statements filed under this Chapter for immoral, unlawful, or unauthorized commercial purposes.

Article F — Conflict of Interest and Divestment

This Article establishes conflict-of-interest avoidance policies and mandatory divestment requirements applicable to local officials and employees.

A local official or employee shall avoid conflict of interest at all times. When a conflict of interest arises, the official or employee shall resign from positions in private business enterprises within thirty (30) days from assumption of office and/or divest himself of shareholdings or interests within sixty (60) days.

Exemption Coverage

Mandatory divestment requirements shall not apply to persons serving in honorary capacity nor to laborers, casual workers, or temporary workers.

Article G — Penalties

This Article governs administrative, civil, and criminal liabilities arising from violations of public ethics, transparency requirements, and conflict-of-interest regulations.

Administrative Penalties

Violations may be punished by fine, suspension, or removal depending on the gravity of the offense.

Application of Heavier Penalties

If another law imposes heavier penalties, prosecution shall proceed under that law.

Violations of Articles D, E, and F

Violations shall be prosecuted under Republic Act No. 6713 or other applicable laws.

Administrative Proceedings

Administrative liability may exist even without criminal prosecution.

Private Individuals

Private persons participating as accomplices or conspirators shall bear corresponding liabilities under applicable laws.

Article H — Determining and Defining the Parameters or Duties, Functions and Powers of the Respective Municipal Government Department Heads

This Article establishes the operational mandates, powers, responsibilities, and governance functions of municipal department heads and administrative offices.
  • Advise the Mayor, Sanggunian, and officials regarding local government funds and public finance.
  • Take custody and proper management of municipal funds.
  • Administer lawful disbursement of entrusted funds.
  • Inspect establishments in relation to implementation of tax ordinances.
  • Maintain and update the municipal tax information system.
  • Perform duties prescribed by law and ordinance.
  • Ensure implementation of laws governing appraisal and assessment of real properties.

  • Maintain property identification and accounting systems.
  • Conduct physical surveys of taxable properties.
  • Prepare schedules of fair market value.
  • Issue certified assessment records upon lawful request.
  • Attend sessions of the Local Board of Assessment Appeals.

Assistant Assessor

The Municipal Mayor may appoint an Assistant Assessor subject to Civil Service laws and prescribed qualification standards.

  • Install and maintain internal audit systems.
  • Prepare and submit financial statements.
  • Certify budgetary allotments and obligations.
  • Review supporting financial documents and vouchers.
  • Maintain accounting ledgers, payroll records, and disbursement systems.
  • Maintain records involving municipal property and obligations.
  • Prepare budgetary forms, orders, and circulars.
  • Review and consolidate budget proposals.
  • Assist during budget preparation and hearings.
  • Evaluate budgetary implications of proposed legislation.
  • Coordinate with treasury, accounting, and planning offices.
  • Assist the Sangguniang Bayan in reviewing barangay budgets.
  • Formulate integrated economic, social, and physical development plans.
  • Conduct studies, research, and development planning programs.
  • Coordinate sectoral development studies and plans.
  • Monitor and evaluate municipal development projects.
  • Promote public participation in local planning processes.
  • Exercise supervision over the Local Development Council secretariat.
  • Develop infrastructure policies, plans, and programs.
  • Advise the Mayor regarding engineering and public works matters.
  • Supervise construction, repair, and maintenance of infrastructure projects.
  • Provide engineering services including surveys, feasibility studies, and project management.
  • Supervise municipal health services and personnel.
  • Develop health programs, policies, and implementation guidelines.
  • Advise the Mayor and Sanggunian on health matters.
  • Enforce laws and ordinances relating to public health.
  • Conduct health information campaigns and intelligence services.
  • Coordinate with government agencies and NGOs on health service delivery.
  • Serve in frontline health response during disasters and calamities.
  • Develop civil registry plans and programs.
  • Accept and preserve registrable documents and judicial decrees.
  • Maintain official civil registry books and records.
  • Issue certified transcripts and copies of registered documents.
  • Process marriage license applications subject to legal requirements.
  • Coordinate demographic and statistical activities with national agencies.
  • Provide technical assistance in agricultural services and development.
  • Develop agricultural programs and strategies.
  • Support farmers, fishermen, and local entrepreneurs.
  • Assist in demonstration farms and aquaculture development.
  • Coordinate with agencies promoting agricultural productivity.

  • Develop social welfare and development programs.
  • Provide assistance to disadvantaged and impoverished sectors.
  • Deliver crisis intervention for victims of abuse and exploitation.
  • Implement welfare programs for children, elderly persons, and persons with disabilities.
  • Coordinate with NGOs and government agencies on welfare concerns.
  • Provide immediate relief services during disasters and emergencies.

Strict adherence to the functions and duties specifically provided for in every department is hereby enjoined.

Article I — Setting Up Policies on Travel Order of Municipal Employees

This Article establishes approval procedures and administrative requirements governing official travel orders of municipal employees.
  • All travel orders shall be noted and recommended by respective department heads before approval by the Municipal Mayor.
  • Travel orders shall support payment of lawful travel expenses of employees.

Article J — Annual Mandatory Drug-Test

This Article establishes mandatory annual drug-testing policies applicable to municipal and barangay officials, employees, consultants, and related personnel.

The purpose of this Article is to promote and protect the safety and health of constituents from the harmful effects of dangerous drugs.

Mandatory annual drug-testing shall apply to municipal and barangay officials and employees, whether permanent, temporary, coterminous, consultants, casual, job order, or other personnel performing official functions.

The Municipal Mayor shall determine the schedule and venue of mandatory drug-testing upon recommendation of the Municipal Health Officer and Chief of Police. Barangay schedules shall remain random and unannounced.

Strict confidentiality shall be observed concerning screening procedures and drug-test results pursuant to Republic Act No. 9165.

First Offense

Suspension from one (1) month and one (1) day up to six (6) months.

Second Offense

Dismissal from service.

Penalties for positive drug-test results shall apply in accordance with Civil Service laws, Republic Act No. 9165, the Local Government Code, and related regulations.

Coverage of Penalties

Separate penalties apply to appointed barangay personnel, appointed municipal personnel, and elected officials. Mandatory rehabilitation, suspension, dismissal, administrative liability, and criminal prosecution may apply depending on circumstances and repeat violations.

Drug-testing expenses for municipal personnel shall be funded by the Municipality of Santa, Ilocos Sur subject to annual appropriations.

The Municipal Mayor may initiate or direct filing of criminal and administrative actions for violations of this Article.

The Municipal Mayor may contract accredited drug-testing agencies subject to confirmation by the Sangguniang Bayan.

The Municipal Mayor, Municipal Chief of Police, Municipal Health Officer, and Liga ng mga Barangay President shall formulate implementing rules and regulations within three (3) months from effectivity.

Article K — LGU-Santa Legal Assistance Fund

This Article establishes the LGU-Santa Legal Assistance Fund for indigent legal assistance, legal education, and partnership programs.

This Article shall be known as the “LGU-Santa Legal Assistance Fund.”

The Municipality recognizes its duty to support access to free legal assistance and strengthen partnerships with organizations promoting public welfare and justice.

One Hundred Thousand Pesos (Php100,000.00) from unexpended funds shall be realigned for the Legal Assistance Fund.

Funds shall support free legal aid for indigent constituents and legal seminars, trainings, and workshops within the Municipality.

The Local Chief Executive is authorized to enter into a Memorandum of Agreement with the IBP-Ilocos Sur for implementation of the Legal Assistance Fund program.

The Office of the Mayor, Municipal Accountant, and Integrated Bar of the Philippines shall formulate implementing rules and regulations for proper implementation.

IBP-Ilocos Sur shall render regular utilization reports including findings and recommendations regarding the Legal Assistance Fund.

All inconsistent ordinances, resolutions, circulars, memoranda, rules, and regulations are repealed or modified accordingly.

If any provision of this Article is declared unconstitutional or invalid, remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

Article L — Public Employment Service Office (PESO)

This Article institutionalizes the Public Employment Service Office (PESO) to promote employment facilitation, labor market coordination, reintegration services, and public employment programs.

This Article shall be known as the “PESO Ordinance of Santa, Ilocos Sur.”

The Municipality shall promote full employment and equality of employment opportunities and institutionalize the Department of Labor and Employment National Employment Facilitation Service Network through the PESO.

A Public Employment Service Office (PESO) shall be established under the administrative supervision of the Office of the Mayor.

The PESO shall ensure efficient delivery of employment facilitation services, labor market information, referrals, entrepreneurial reintegration, counseling, and related services.

  • Coordinate reintegration services for returning overseas workers.
  • Submit annual employment plans and budgetary requirements.
  • Promote youth employment programs including SPES and GIP.
  • Facilitate labor market information exchange.
  • Provide livelihood and self-employment referrals.
  • Conduct employment counseling and career guidance.
  • Perform functions necessary to carry out PESO objectives.

The PESO shall initially be composed of a PESO Manager and may be assisted by a Labor and Employment Officer as determined by the Local Government Unit.

Funding for implementation of the PESO shall be included in the annual municipal budget subject to accounting and auditing rules.

If any provision is declared unconstitutional or invalid, remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

All inconsistent ordinances, executive orders, rules, and regulations are repealed or modified accordingly.

Article M — People’s Law Enforcement Board (PLEB)

This Article creates the People’s Law Enforcement Board (PLEB) and establishes its composition, jurisdiction, compensation, replacement procedures, and funding mechanisms.
  • One member of the Sangguniang Bayan chosen by the Sanggunian.
  • One representative Punong Barangay chosen by the Liga ng mga Barangay.
  • Three respected community members chosen by the Municipal Peace and Order Council.

Members of the PLEB shall serve for three (3) years from assumption of office until successors are chosen and qualified.

The PLEB shall exercise jurisdiction over citizens’ complaints and related matters pursuant to Republic Act No. 6975 and other applicable issuances.

PLEB membership is a civic duty. Members and the secretary shall receive honoraria based on actual attendance subject to limitations prescribed by this Article.

Vacancies, resignations, removals, or inability to perform duties shall be addressed through replacement or reorganization by the Municipal Mayor.

Funding requirements for PLEB operations shall be included in the annual municipal budget appropriation.

If any provision is declared unlawful or invalid, remaining provisions shall remain effective.

Article N — Official Seal of the Sangguniang Bayan

This Article adopts the Official Seal of the Sangguniang Bayan, regulates its lawful use, establishes custodial responsibilities, and prescribes penalties for violations.

This Article shall be known as the ordinance adopting the Official Seal of the Sangguniang Bayan of Santa, Ilocos Sur.

The Official Seal of the Sangguniang Bayan is hereby adopted as its official symbol.

  • Red symbolizes courage, resiliency, and action.
  • Blue symbolizes security, loyalty, and responsibility.
  • The feather quill symbolizes legislative functions.
  • The stars and sun symbolize allegiance to the Republic of the Philippines.
  • White symbolizes purity and loftiness of intentions.

Only authorized officials and employees of the Sangguniang Bayan may use the Official Seal for lawful official purposes.

The Office of the Sangguniang Bayan shall maintain custody and safekeeping of the original lay-out and official electronic copies of the Official Seal.

  • Forging, counterfeiting, or unlawfully affixing the Official Seal.
  • Defacing, mutilating, ridiculing, or casting contempt upon the Official Seal.
  • Using the Official Seal for unauthorized advertisements or commercial activities.
  • Adding unauthorized words, marks, figures, or designs on the Official Seal.
  • Using the Official Seal as a personal seal of any public official.
  • Using or affixing the Official Seal without proper authority.

Changes in the design of the Official Seal may only be made for justifiable reasons through unanimous vote of all members of the Sangguniang Bayan, provided that changes shall not occur more than once every ten (10) years.

Official carved seals of the Sangguniang Bayan shall be permanently displayed at the Office of the Municipal Vice Mayor and the Session Hall.

The Office of the Sangguniang Bayan shall maintain an official dry seal for authentication of public documents and administrative issuances. The Secretary of the Sangguniang Bayan shall exercise custody and safekeeping of the dry seal and ensure that its use is limited only to official functions and office hours.

Unauthorized Use and Counterfeiting

Violations involving unauthorized use, counterfeiting, or misuse of the Official Seal may be punished by fine, imprisonment, or both.

Other Violations

Other violations may result in corresponding fines, imprisonment, or both depending on the gravity of the offense.

Administrative Liability

Public officers or employees violating this Article may incur administrative liability without prejudice to civil or criminal actions.

If any provision of this Article is declared unconstitutional or invalid, the remaining provisions not affected thereby shall remain in full force and effect.

All ordinances or parts thereof inconsistent with this Article are hereby repealed or modified accordingly.
