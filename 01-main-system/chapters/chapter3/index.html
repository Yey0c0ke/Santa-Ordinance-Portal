Article A — Declaration of Policy

This Article establishes the municipal government’s commitment to ethical public service, accountability, integrity, professionalism, and public-interest-centered governance.

It is the policy of this municipal government to promote a high standard of ethics in public service. All local officials and employees shall at all times be accountable to the people and shall discharge their duties with utmost responsibility, integrity, competence and loyalty; act with patriotism and justice; lead modest lives and uphold public interest over personal interest.

Public Officials

Include elective and appointive officials and employees, permanent or temporary, whether in the career or non-career service, including military and police personnel, whether or not they receive compensation.

Gift

Refers to a thing or right disposed of gratuitously and includes simulated sales or liberality, excluding unsolicited gifts of nominal value not connected with favors.

Receiving Any Gift

Includes accepting directly or indirectly any gift beyond nominal value in anticipation of or in exchange for a favor.

Loan

Covers simple loans, commodatum, guarantees, financing arrangements, or accommodations intended to ensure approval.

Substantial Stockholder

Means a person who directly or indirectly owns sufficient shares to elect a director of a corporation.

Family of Local Officials or Employees

Refers to spouses and unmarried children below eighteen (18) years of age.

Conflict of Interest

Arises when private business interests conflict with the faithful performance of official duties.

Divestment

Means the transfer or disposal of interest in property by completely depriving oneself of title or ownership.

Relatives

Include persons related within the fourth civil degree of consanguinity or affinity including bilas, pakaisa, and balae.

Article B — Norms of Conduct of Local Officials and Employees

This Article establishes the standards of personal conduct, professionalism, neutrality, accountability, patriotism, and ethical public service expected from all local officials and employees.

Commitment to Public Interest

Government resources and powers shall be used efficiently, honestly, effectively, and economically in service of the public.

Professionalism

Officials and employees shall discharge duties with excellence, intelligence, competence, and skill.

Fairness and Sincerity

Officials shall act with justness and sincerity, avoid discrimination, and refrain from unlawful or immoral acts.

Political Neutrality

Public service shall be rendered without unfair discrimination regardless of political affiliation.

Responsiveness to the Public

Officials shall provide prompt, courteous, transparent, and efficient public service while avoiding red tape.

Nationalism and Patriotism

Officials shall promote Filipino interests, sovereignty, and locally produced resources and technologies.

Commitment to Democracy

Officials shall uphold constitutional principles, public accountability, and civilian supremacy.

Simple Living

Officials and employees shall lead modest lives appropriate to their positions and lawful income.

Article C — Duties and Responsibilities of Local Officials and Employees

This Article establishes the operational obligations, administrative responsibilities, responsiveness standards, and document-processing duties of local officials and employees.
  • Act promptly on letters, requests, and official communications within fifteen (15) working days.
  • Submit annual performance reports within forty-five (45) working days after the end of the year.
  • Process official papers and documents expeditiously and efficiently.
  • Attend promptly to public transactions and requests for government services.
  • Ensure accessibility of public documents for inspection during reasonable working hours.

Administrative Accountability

The Article institutionalizes responsiveness, transparency, efficiency, and procedural accountability within municipal governance operations.

Article D — Ethical Standards

This Article governs prohibited acts, conflict-related transactions, misuse of confidential information, solicitation of gifts, and ethical restrictions imposed upon local officials and employees.

Financial and Material Interest

Officials and employees shall not possess financial or material interests in transactions requiring approval of their office.

Outside Employment

Officials shall not manage or accept employment in private enterprises regulated or supervised by their office unless authorized by law.

Practice of Profession

Practice of profession is prohibited where conflicts with official duties may arise, subject to existing laws.

Recommendation to Private Enterprises

Officials shall not recommend persons to private entities with pending or regular transactions with their office.

Disclosure of Confidential Information

Confidential or classified information shall not be used for private gain or to prejudice public interest.

Solicitation or Acceptance of Gifts

Officials and employees shall not solicit or accept gifts, favors, loans, gratuities, or entertainment connected with official duties.

Foreign Government Gifts

Acceptance of nominal souvenirs, scholarships, medical treatment, and approved travel grants from foreign governments may be allowed under lawful conditions.

Article E — Transparency in Public Service

This Article establishes transparency mechanisms, SALN requirements, disclosure obligations, accessibility of public documents, and safeguards against misuse of government records.

All local officials and employees, except those serving in honorary capacity, laborers, casuals, or temporary employees, shall file under oath their Statement of Assets, Liabilities and Net Worth (SALN), including disclosure of business interests and financial connections.

Real Property

Includes improvements, acquisition costs, assessed value, and fair market value.

Personal Property

Includes movable assets and acquisition costs.

Financial Connections

Includes business interests, investments, and affiliations requiring disclosure.

Public Accessibility

SALN records shall remain available for public inspection subject to lawful procedures and restrictions.

Public documents shall be made accessible to the public during official working hours except those classified as confidential by law or regulation.

Article F — Conflict of Interest and Divestment

This Article establishes restrictions concerning conflicts of interest, divestment requirements, prohibited financial participation, and mandatory ethical separation between public duties and private interests.

A conflict of interest arises when a local official or employee is a substantial stockholder, owner, or has substantial interest in a business which may be affected by official action.

Ethical Separation Principle

Public office shall not be used directly or indirectly to secure private advantage, preferential treatment, or economic benefit.

Officials and employees shall divest themselves of interests requiring intervention by their office within the period prescribed by law.

Article G — Penalties

This Article governs administrative, civil, and criminal liabilities arising from violations of ethical standards, disclosure obligations, prohibited transactions, and misconduct in public office.
  • Suspension from office.
  • Dismissal from service.
  • Forfeiture of benefits.
  • Disqualification from holding public office.

Violations may give rise to criminal prosecution under applicable national laws, anti-graft statutes, and administrative regulations.

Article H — Municipal Department Heads

This Article governs the organizational functions, operational responsibilities, administrative supervision, and accountability of municipal department heads.
  • Administer departmental operations.
  • Supervise personnel and internal systems.
  • Implement municipal policies and ordinances.
  • Prepare departmental reports and recommendations.
  • Coordinate with local and national government agencies.

Department heads shall remain administratively accountable for lawful, efficient, transparent, and responsive implementation of municipal programs and operations.

Article I — Travel Order Policies

This Article establishes procedures governing travel authorization, official representation, accountability for public expenses, and approval systems for official travel.

No official or employee shall travel on official business without an approved travel order issued by the proper authority.

Municipal Mayor

Approves travel orders of municipal employees and department heads subject to applicable regulations.

Sangguniang Bayan

Travel involving legislative functions may require approval consistent with internal legislative procedures.

Supporting Documents

Travel requests shall include purpose, itinerary, duration, and estimated expenses.

Article J — Mandatory Drug Testing

This Article establishes mandatory drug-testing policies intended to promote integrity, discipline, public trust, and professionalism within the municipal government workforce.

Mandatory drug testing may be conducted upon municipal officials and employees in accordance with applicable laws, civil service regulations, and lawful administrative procedures.

Drug-testing records and medical information shall remain confidential except when disclosure is authorized by law or required by competent authority.

Article K — Legal Assistance Fund

This Article establishes legal assistance mechanisms intended to support indigent litigants, lawful municipal interests, and authorized legal representation programs.
  • Legal consultations and representation assistance.
  • Support for indigent litigants.
  • Municipal legal defense support.
  • Legal coordination with accredited authorities.

Funding may be sourced from appropriations, grants, lawful donations, and other authorized municipal funding mechanisms.

Article L — Public Employment Service Office

This Article governs the establishment, functions, and operational responsibilities of the Public Employment Service Office (PESO).
  • Facilitate local employment matching services.
  • Provide labor market information.
  • Coordinate livelihood and employment programs.
  • Assist applicants seeking local and overseas employment.
  • Coordinate with national employment agencies.

Employment assistance services shall remain accessible, responsive, and nondiscriminatory to qualified applicants.

Article M — People’s Law Enforcement Board

This Article governs the composition, jurisdiction, disciplinary authority, and procedural operations of the People’s Law Enforcement Board (PLEB).

Chairperson

A duly designated representative consistent with governing laws and local regulations.

Community Representatives

Representatives from civic, religious, or professional sectors may participate as authorized.

Jurisdiction

PLEB exercises disciplinary authority over matters lawfully delegated under applicable statutes.

  • Hear administrative complaints.
  • Conduct lawful proceedings and investigations.
  • Recommend disciplinary sanctions.
  • Promote accountability within law enforcement services.

Article N — Official Seal of the Sangguniang Bayan

This Article governs the official seal of the Sangguniang Bayan, including lawful usage, preservation, authentication authority, and penalties for unauthorized use.

The Sangguniang Bayan shall maintain an official seal for authentication of ordinances, resolutions, certifications, and official legislative records.

Unauthorized reproduction, misuse, falsification, or unlawful possession of the official seal shall subject responsible persons to lawful administrative, civil, or criminal liability.
