ARTICLE A

Possession of Firecrackers

Amending Municipal Ordinance No. 3, Series of 1980.

No person shall possess any destructive firecrackers irrespective of quantities within the jurisdiction of this municipality.

During special occasions like fiestas, coronation rites, Christmas and New Year's Eve where the need to use firecrackers becomes necessary, the mayor may issue a special permit for possession and use of such materials, provided that the permittee or user thereof is eighteen (18) years of age.

Firecracker means fireworks made of paper in cylinder form or triangle or whatever form enclosing an explosive material and similar explosives which are destructive to lives and properties.

Possession includes exclusive ownership or control of firecrackers as defined.

Sales means for and in consideration of an amount, cause the transfer of any quantity of firecrackers to an individual.

All ordinances, rules and regulations or parts thereof, in conflict or inconsistent with any provisions of this article are hereby repealed or modified accordingly.

Any person who violates the provisions of this article shall be fined not less than Five Hundred (P500.00) Pesos but not more than One Thousand (P1,000.00) Pesos or imprisonment of three (3) days to ten (10) days or both, at the discretion of the court.

A. Firecrackers:

1. Baby Rocket
2. Small Triangulo
3. Pulling of Strings
4. Paper Caps
5. El Diablo
6. Judah’s Belt
7. Other equivalent types

B. Pyrotechnic Devices:

1. Sparklers
2. Luces
3. Fountain
4. Jumbo regular and special
5. Mabuhay
6. Roman candle
7. Trompillo
8. Airwolf
9. Whistle Device
10. Butterfly
11. Pallaw devices
12. Other equivalent devices

The sale, distribution and use of any firecrackers and pyrotechnic devices not mentioned in Section 4A.07, with explosive content that could endanger life and limb, are strictly prohibited.

a) License and permit to sell and distribute firecrackers and pyrotechnic devices shall only be granted in accordance with this Article;

b) No license shall be issued for prohibited firecrackers;

c) Wholesalers, dealers and retailers shall comply with all requirements of the Firearms and Explosives Division of the Philippine National Police;

d) A Special Business Permit shall be required.

a) Corporations or partnerships must be at least sixty percent (60%) Filipino-owned;

b) Single proprietors must be Filipino citizens of good moral character;

c) Must be registered with the Department of Trade and Industry;

d) Must be certified by the Bureau of Fire Protection;

e) Permits shall be non-transferable.

a) Selling shall only be allowed in designated zones approved by the Local Chief Executive;

b) No testing or use shall be allowed within selling zones;

c) Selling hours shall be regulated;

d) Inventory shall be limited and properly stored;

e) Selling to minors is prohibited;

f) Minors are prohibited from handling or using firecrackers.

The use of firecrackers and pyrotechnic devices shall be strictly prohibited in residential areas.

Any person or entity desiring to use firecrackers for cultural or traditional purposes must secure a permit from the municipality.

Any person or entity who violates any provision of this Article shall be punished by:

• A fine of Two Thousand Five Hundred Pesos (₱2,500.00); or
• Imprisonment not exceeding thirty (30) days; or
• Both such fine and imprisonment at the discretion of the court.

In addition, violations shall result in cancellation of business permit and confiscation of inventory.

ARTICLE B

Public Safety Measures During Local Celebrations

In order to avoid the occurrence of accidents which the inhabitants are exposed to during local celebrations such as festival, fiesta, fairs, carnivals, parades and processions, the following guidelines are hereby adopted:

1. On Road Safety — No person shall be allowed to park a motor vehicle on any sidewalk, park or alley along the route of the parades or processions during local celebrations.

2. On Torches — No person shall be allowed to use torches during religious processions and parades of any kind, except the Boy Scouts and Girl Scouts of the Philippines during their Jamboree Sessions.

3. On Platforms and Stages — Only sturdy materials shall be used in the construction of floats, platforms and stages.

Sturdy materials — any wood material or any other of similar nature.

Any violation of the provisions of this article shall be fined One Thousand Pesos (Php 1,000.00) but not more than Two Thousand Five Hundred Pesos (Php 2,500.00) or imprisonment of one (1) month or both at the discretion of the court.

ARTICLE C

Flammable & Combustible Materials Regulation

No person or entity shall store, keep, sell, transport, or distribute flammable, combustible, or explosive materials within the territorial jurisdiction of the Municipality of Santa without securing the necessary permit and complying with all fire safety regulations.

Covered materials include:

• Gasoline
• Diesel
• Kerosene
• LPG
• Fireworks
• Chemicals
• Industrial solvents
• Other highly combustible substances

All storage facilities must comply with:

a) Bureau of Fire Protection regulations;

b) Proper ventilation systems;

c) Safe distance from residential areas, schools, churches, and public facilities;

d) Proper fire suppression systems and emergency exits.

The Municipal Government, through the Bureau of Fire Protection and other authorized agencies, shall conduct regular inspections to ensure compliance with this Article.

Any violation of this Article shall be punishable by:

• Fine of not less than ₱1,000.00 but not exceeding ₱2,500.00
• Suspension or revocation of permit
• Closure of establishment when public safety is endangered

ARTICLE D

Kites & Balloon Restrictions

Flying of kites, balloons, and similar airborne objects shall be prohibited in areas where electric lines, communication facilities, transportation systems, and public safety may be affected.

It shall be unlawful to use metallic, flammable, or electrically conductive materials in kite strings or airborne devices.

No person shall fly kites or balloons in such a manner that may obstruct:

• Traffic movement
• Electrical systems
• Public transportation
• Aviation safety
• Emergency operations

Violators shall be fined not less than ₱500.00 but not more than ₱2,500.00 and/or imprisonment not exceeding thirty (30) days at the discretion of the court.

ARTICLE E

Anti-Prank Calls Ordinance

No person shall make prank, false, misleading, or malicious emergency calls to:

• Police stations
• Fire stations
• Rescue centers
• Hospitals
• Municipal emergency response units

The municipality recognizes that prank emergency calls waste government resources, delay emergency response, and place lives and properties in danger.

Any person found guilty of violating this Article shall be punished by:

• Fine of ₱2,500.00
• Imprisonment not exceeding thirty (30) days
• Or both at the discretion of the court

ARTICLE F

Restriction on Sale of Intoxicating Drinks to Minors

No person, store, restaurant, beer house, sari-sari store, grocery, or business establishment shall sell, serve, or distribute intoxicating beverages to minors.

This Article applies to:

• Restaurants
• Beer houses
• Groceries
• Sari-sari stores
• Supermarkets
• Convenience stores
• Other commercial establishments selling alcoholic beverages

Covered establishments shall display visible notices stating that intoxicating drinks shall not be sold to minors.

Violators shall be punished by:

• First Offense — ₱1,000.00
• Second Offense — ₱1,500.00 and suspension of permit
• Third Offense — ₱2,000.00 and cancellation of business permit

ARTICLE H

Students & Recreation Hall Restrictions

Students and minors are prohibited from entering billiard halls, computer shops, videoke houses, and similar recreational establishments during school hours unless accompanied by parents, guardians, or authorized school officials.

Operators and owners of recreational establishments are responsible for ensuring that minors and students are not admitted during restricted hours.

All establishments covered by this Article shall display visible notices informing the public of the restriction on minors.

Violators shall be fined:

• First Offense — ₱500.00
• Second Offense — ₱1,000.00
• Third Offense — Suspension or revocation of permit

ARTICLE I

Public Drinking Regulation

No person shall drink or consume intoxicating beverages in:

• Streets
• Sidewalks
• Parks
• Public plazas
• Government premises
• Public terminals
• Other public places

The municipality seeks to preserve peace, order, cleanliness, and public discipline by regulating public drinking activities.

Violators shall be punished with:

• Fine of ₱500.00 to ₱2,500.00
• Community service
• Or imprisonment at the discretion of the court

ARTICLE L

Public Urination Prohibition

Urinating in public places such as streets, alleys, parks, vacant lots, sidewalks, government properties, and similar areas is strictly prohibited.

This Article aims to preserve cleanliness, public sanitation, health standards, and municipal discipline.

Any violation shall be punishable by:

• Fine of ₱500.00
• Community service
• Or imprisonment not exceeding thirty (30) days

ARTICLE M

Half-Nude Public Roaming Restriction

No person shall roam, walk, stay, or loiter in public places within the Municipality while half-nude or without upper garments.

This prohibition applies to:

• Streets
• Public roads
• Markets
• Government premises
• Public terminals
• Commercial establishments
• Other public places

Violators shall be punished by:

• Fine of ₱500.00
• Community service
• Or imprisonment not exceeding thirty (30) days

ARTICLE N

Basketball Court Parking Prohibition

Parking of motorcycles, tricycles, cars, trucks, and other vehicles inside municipal and barangay basketball courts is strictly prohibited unless authorized during official municipal activities.

Basketball courts are intended for sports, community activities, and emergency public use and shall not be converted into long-term parking areas.

Violators shall be fined:

• First Offense — ₱300.00
• Second Offense — ₱500.00
• Third Offense — ₱1,000.00

ARTICLE O

Inspection & Survey Fees

The municipality shall impose fees for ocular inspections, real property verification, site validation, and municipal assessment activities.

Inspection and survey fees may apply to:

• Real property assessments
• Land verification
• Business inspections
• Site evaluations
• Building compliance activities

All fees imposed under this Article shall be paid to the Municipal Treasurer prior to the conduct of official inspection or survey activities.

ARTICLE P

Tricycle Regulation System

All tricycles operating within the Municipality of Santa shall comply with municipal transport regulations, registration systems, franchise rules, and operational requirements.

Tricycles shall display:

• Municipal stickers
• Identification numbers
• Franchise markings
• Color coding systems
• Plate information

Drivers and operators shall comply with:

• Authorized routes
• Fare regulations
• Passenger safety measures
• Municipal transport standards

Violators may be subjected to:

• Fines
• Suspension of franchise
• Revocation of permit
• Vehicle impounding

ARTICLE S

Cockpit & Cockfighting Regulation

Cockpit operations and cockfighting activities within the Municipality shall be regulated by municipal permits, licensing requirements, safety standards, and lawful operating schedules.

No cockpit operator shall conduct cockfighting activities without securing the necessary municipal permits and complying with all applicable laws.

Any unauthorized cockpit activity shall result in:

• Fines
• Suspension of permit
• Closure of operation
• Other penalties authorized by law

ARTICLE T

Weights & Measures Regulation

All weighing scales, measuring devices, and commercial measurement instruments used within the Municipality shall undergo official calibration and inspection.

The Municipality shall ensure protection against fraudulent weighing and measuring practices affecting consumers and businesses.

Any person found using defective, tampered, or uncalibrated measuring instruments shall be penalized in accordance with municipal regulations and national law.

ARTICLE U

Mandatory Child Protection Notices

This Ordinance shall be known as the Mandatory Posting of Notice to Minors Ordinance of the Municipality of Santa.

This Article applies to all establishments whose operations may prejudice the development and welfare of minors.

All covered establishments shall:

• Post notices at entrances and front desks
• Use a minimum size of 2 ft × 2 ft
• Include ordinance number and title
• Protect notices from removal, damage, or defacement

Required Notices:

a) Hotels, Lodging Houses, Beer Houses, Videoke Bars → “Minors are strictly not allowed inside”

b) Supermarkets, Groceries, Restaurants, Carinderias, Bake Shops → “This establishment is not allowed to sell tobacco products and intoxicating drinks to minors”

c) Hardware & Construction Supplies → “This establishment is not allowed to sell rugby and habit-inducing solvents to minors”

d) Amusement Centers → “Minors are not allowed from 7:30 AM to 5:00 PM, Monday to Friday”

e) Lotto Outlets → “Minors are not allowed to bet”

f) Cockpit Arena → “Minors are not allowed to enter”

g) Drugstores → “This establishment is not allowed to sell prescription drugs to minors”

The Municipal Treasurer’s Office, in coordination with the MSWDO, Municipal Health Office, and PNP, shall conduct inspections before permit issuance, renewal, and periodic compliance checks.

First Offense — ₱1,000.00
Second Offense — ₱1,500.00 and one month suspension
Third Offense — ₱2,000.00 and permanent cancellation

ARTICLE W

Benefit Events Regulation

No person, association, or fraternity shall conduct benefit dances, shows, raffle draws, bingo, or analogous activities without securing a permit from the Office of the Mayor.

Applications shall be submitted at least one (1) day before the scheduled activity and shall only proceed upon approval of the Municipal Mayor.

Residents — ₱300.00
Non-residents — ₱500.00

Any violation shall be punished by:

• Fine of ₱2,500.00
• Imprisonment not exceeding thirty (30) days
• Or both at the discretion of the court

ARTICLE X

Astray Animals Regulation

Astray animals such as cows, carabaos, horses, and goats are strictly prohibited from roaming within the territorial jurisdiction of the Municipality.

Covered animals shall be kept in corrals and properly restrained while grazing.

Owners of animals found astray shall pay the following fines:

• Cow/Carabao/Horse — ₱500.00
• Goat — ₱200.00

A catcher’s fee shall be imposed as follows:

• Cow/Carabao/Horse — 30% of fine
• Goat — 25% of fine

• Cow/Carabao/Horse — ₱100/day
• Goat — ₱50/day

ARTICLE Y

Anti-Pirated Goods Regulation

No person or business establishment shall engage in the sale, rental, transfer, distribution, manufacture, or production of pirated, counterfeit, or fake goods, counterfeit drugs, or pharmaceutical products.

All business permits issued by the municipality shall carry the condition that the licensee shall not engage in prohibited counterfeit-related activities.

Violations shall result in:

• Suspension of business permit
• Revocation of permit
• Cancellation for repeated violations

Any violation shall be punished by:

• Fine of ₱2,500.00
• Imprisonment not exceeding six (6) months
• Or both at the discretion of the court

ARTICLE Z

Pre-Emptive & Forced Evacuation

It is the declared policy of the Municipal Government of Santa, Ilocos Sur to protect the lives and properties of residents and mitigate the effects of disasters and emergencies.

This Article defines:

• Disaster
• Evacuation
• Forced Evacuation
• Imminent Danger
• Incident Commander
• Pre-emptive Evacuation

Upon determination of the Incident Commander that pre-emptive evacuation is necessary, affected residents shall immediately be evacuated.

The Municipal Mayor may declare forced evacuation upon recommendation of the Incident Commander and mobilize evacuation centers, transportation, security, animal custody, and emergency systems.

The PNP, AFP, PCG, BFP, and Municipal Engineer’s Office may be mobilized during implementation of forced evacuation.

Reasonable force may be used to ensure evacuation of residents when necessary for public safety and preservation of life.

The MDRRMO shall conduct annual trainings, orientations, and public information campaigns regarding disaster preparedness and evacuation procedures.

This ordinance shall take effect immediately upon approval.

ARTICLE AA

Videoke Machines Regulation

Users of videoke machines and similar electronic devices must first secure a permit from the barangay at least one (1) day before use.

The use of videoke systems shall only be allowed until 11:00 PM and must maintain tolerable listening levels.

Use of videoke systems within fifty (50) meters from churches, schools, hospitals, and religious sites is prohibited unless allowable sound limits are maintained.

Second Offense — ₱500.00
Third Offense — ₱1,000.00 and/or imprisonment of six (6) days or community service of forty-eight (48) hours.

ARTICLE AB

Municipal Dangerous Drugs Administrative Board

The Municipal Dangerous Drugs Administrative Board shall be composed of:

• Municipal Health Officer — Chairman
• President, Liga ng mga Barangay — Member
• Chief of Police — Member

The Board shall:

• Receive complaints on drug-related nuisances
• Conduct hearings
• Declare premises as drug-related nuisances
• Abate dangerous drug-related activities

Upon declaration of a drug-related public nuisance, the Board shall issue orders prohibiting activities contributing to such nuisance and the Municipal Mayor shall enforce the same.

ARTICLE AC

Responsible Disaster Warning Ordinance

The Municipality shall ensure dissemination of accurate, reliable, and timely information during disasters and emergency situations.

The following are prohibited:

• Disseminating false disaster information
• Publishing fabricated emergency reports
• Sharing misleading disaster-related content causing panic or confusion

Violators shall be penalized with:

• Fine of ₱1,000.00 to ₱2,500.00
• Imprisonment of ten (10) to thirty (30) days
• Or both at the discretion of the court

ARTICLE AD

Municipal Anti-Idling Ordinance

Idling of gasoline or diesel-powered motor vehicles for more than five (5) minutes in public or crowded areas is prohibited.

Exemptions include:

• Traffic congestion
• Emergency vehicles
• Official inspections
• Armored vehicles under guard

First Offense — ₱500.00
Succeeding Offense — ₱1,000.00

ARTICLE AE

Children & Youth Emergency DRRM Program

The municipality seeks to inculcate among children and youth the importance of disaster preparedness, community safety, and emergency response participation.

The program covers:

• Public and private schools
• Twenty-six (26) barangays
• Children and youth organizations

Training includes:

• Basic Rescue Operations
• CPR
• First Aid
• Earthquake Drills
• Tsunami Drills
• Fire Drills
• Disaster Preparedness Lectures

Outstanding youth participants shall form part of the “Santa Batang Rescuers” under the LDRRMO core group.

ARTICLE AF

Motion Sensor Alarm Systems

This Article applies to all pawnshops and banks operating within the Municipality of Santa, Ilocos Sur.

Banks and pawnshops are required to install motion sensor alarm systems in addition to existing vault security systems.

Non-compliance shall result in:

• Fine of ₱2,500.00
• Revocation or cancellation of business permit

ARTICLE AG

Regulated Parking System

Parking areas and transportation terminals shall be regulated along the commercial lane of the national highway, particularly near the public market and designated municipal areas.

Parking of public utility vehicles shall be prohibited along:

• Commercial lanes of the national highway
• Municipal hall vicinity
• Loading/unloading restricted zones
• Officially designated no-parking areas

Commercial vehicles may only load and unload within authorized areas and only for limited periods of time.

Violators shall pay a fine of ₱500.00.

• 30% shall accrue to the concerned barangay
• 70% shall accrue to municipal coffers

ARTICLE AH

Motorcycle Lights On Ordinance

All motorcycle operators traversing roads, highways, and streets within the Municipality shall keep headlights and tail lights turned on at all times.

Headlights shall only be white or yellowish in color and defective lights must immediately be replaced.

Violators shall be fined ₱500.00.

ARTICLE AI

Slow Moving Vehicles Ordinance

The Municipality seeks to ensure public safety and convenience by regulating the use of outer lanes and road shoulders for slow-moving vehicles, processions, motorcycles, tricycles, and bicycles.

It shall be unlawful to:

• Obstruct outer lanes
• Convert roads into solar dryers
• Use road shoulders for illegal parking
• Convert rights-of-way into private use

• First Offense — ₱300.00
• Second Offense — ₱500.00
• Third Offense — ₱1,000.00
• Recidivist — ₱2,500.00

ARTICLE AJ

Automatic Municipal Curfew

Upon declaration of a state of calamity, public emergency, or extraordinary condition, an automatic curfew shall immediately take effect.

Residents and visitors are prohibited from loitering in public places from:

8:00 PM to 5:00 AM

Authorized Persons Outside Residence (APOR), frontliners, emergency personnel, and authorized travelers shall be exempted.

Violators shall be fined ₱1,000.00.

ARTICLE AK

Mandatory Quarantine Ordinance

Returning residents from high-risk areas shall undergo mandatory quarantine in municipal quarantine facilities subject to DOH and MHO guidelines.

Home quarantine may be allowed for:

• Immunocompromised individuals
• Nursing mothers
• Senior citizens with disabilities
• Persons with special medical conditions

Barangay Health Emergency Response Teams shall assist in monitoring and reporting compliance with quarantine requirements.

The LGU may sequester and transfer violators to quarantine facilities without prejudice to filing of criminal or administrative charges.

ARTICLE AL

Community Mental Health Care Service

The Municipality of Santa affirms its duty to promote, protect, and value the mental well-being of all Santanians through accessible, affordable, high-quality, and culturally appropriate mental health care services.

LGU-Santa Community-Based Mental Health Care Services are hereby institutionalized and integrated into the municipality’s public health care system.

The Rural Health Unit shall provide:

• Psychiatric services
• Psychosocial services
• Neurologic consultation
• Counseling
• Referral systems
• Community mental wellness programs

An LGU-Santa Mental Health Advisory Board shall assist in policy formulation, monitoring, implementation, and community coordination.

Funds necessary for implementation shall be charged against available municipal funds subject to existing accounting and auditing rules.

ARTICLE AM

Municipal Youth Development Fund

The Municipal Youth Development Fund or “Kabataan Fund” is hereby created in the Municipality of Santa, Ilocos Sur.

The LGU shall appropriate not less than one percent (1%) of its annual budget for Municipal Youth Development programs.

The Fund shall support programs involving:

• Health
• Education
• Environment
• Active citizenship
• Governance
• Social inclusion
• Peace-building
• Economic empowerment

ARTICLE AN

Anti-Road Obstruction Ordinance

The use of roads, sidewalks, bridges, parks, and public spaces for commercial, private, or obstructive activities is prohibited.

Covered obstructions include:

• Illegal parking
• Sidewalk vending
• Construction materials on sidewalks
• Illegal structures
• Solar dryers
• Junk storage
• Unauthorized signages

• First Offense — ₱1,000.00
• Second Offense — ₱2,000.00
• Third Offense — ₱2,500.00 and revocation of business permit

ARTICLE AO

EBikes, ETrikes & Electric Vehicles Regulation

This Article regulates the use, classification, operation, parking, speed limits, and public safety standards for EBikes, ETrikes, and similar electric vehicles within the Municipality.

Covered classifications include:

• Personal Mobility Scooters
• Electric Kick Scooters
• Category L1–L7 Electric Vehicles
• Electric Motorcycles

Drivers must:
