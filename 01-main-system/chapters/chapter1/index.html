Article A — Short Title and Scope

Section 1A.01 — Title

This Ordinance shall be known as the CODE OF GENERAL ORDINANCES OF SANTA, ILOCOS SUR.

Explanation

This section establishes the official legal identity of the municipal ordinance code. It formally designates the codified legal framework used by the Municipality of Santa for municipal governance, legislation, enforcement, and ordinance interpretation.

Section 1A.02 — Scope

This code covers all general and special ordinances enacted over the years by the municipality of Santa, Ilocos Sur.

Explanation

This provision defines the coverage and jurisdictional reach of the Code. It clarifies that both general and special municipal ordinances are integrated into the codified system.

Article B — Rules of Construction

Article B establishes the official rules for interpreting and applying the Code of General Ordinances.

Legal Importance

This Article functions as the interpretative framework of the entire municipal legal system. It governs how words, timelines, references, conflicts, and legal provisions must be understood and enforced.

Section 1B.01 — Words and Phrases

Words and phrases embodied in this code but not specifically defined shall have the same meaning as found in legal dictionaries as well as in existing laws.

Explanation

Undefined legal terms must be interpreted using recognized legal dictionaries and applicable laws to maintain consistency and prevent arbitrary interpretation.

Section 1B.02 — Construction of Codal Provisions

Key Interpretation Rules

  • General Rule — words interpreted according to common legal usage.
  • Gender and Number — masculine terms apply to all genders and singular/plural forms.
  • Computation of Time — establishes calculation of legal periods and deadlines.
  • Tenses — present tense may include future application.
  • Conflicting Provisions — determines which provision prevails in legal conflict.

Article C — Definitions

Business

A trade or commercial activity regularly engaged in as means of livelihood involving independent judgment and decision-making.

Tax

An enforced monetary contribution imposed by law to support governmental needs and public services.

License or Permit

A legal authorization granted by competent authority allowing regulated activity or occupation.

Profession

A calling requiring government board or bar examinations such as law, medicine, engineering, and accountancy.
